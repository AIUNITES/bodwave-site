About BodWave

Movement, Measured.
Progress, Structured.

BodWave is the training application layer built on top of the HMN (Human Movement Notation) specification — specifically MNN, the Muscular Neuro Notation sublayer. While MNN defines how to notate any muscle movement with precision using the v2.0 @TAG() format, BodWave defines how to use those notations to build progressive training programs.

The BW1–BW4 scale gives trainers, therapists, and athletes a shared vocabulary for progression — one that travels across sessions, practitioners, and platforms without losing accuracy.

The BodWave Scale

Four Stages, One System

Each stage represents a distinct training objective with specific readiness criteria before progression.

BW1
Mobility Wave
Low intensity · Foundation
Active range of motion, tissue quality, and neuromuscular activation. No load — just movement quality and pain-free range. The starting point for any new muscle target or post-injury return.
Applies to: Post-injury rehab, warm-up phases, new training stimulus introduction, mobility deficits
BW2
Strength Wave
Moderate intensity · Load-bearing
Resistance training against external load. Eccentric control, concentric drive, and isometric holds. The primary phase for hypertrophy, injury prevention, and base strength development.
Applies to: General fitness, post-PT strength rebuilding, hypertrophy programs, functional strength
BW3
Power Wave
High intensity · Speed + Force
Ballistic and plyometric loading. Rate of force development, elasticity, and multi-joint coordination under speed. Requires demonstrated BW2 competency before entry.
Applies to: Athletes, ACL/tendon return-to-sport protocols, explosive movement training
BW4
Performance Wave
Peak intensity · Sport-specific
Full-system output under competitive or performance conditions. Sport-specific demands, fatigue tolerance, and peak neuromuscular recruitment. Reserved for athletes and high-performance contexts.
Applies to: Competitive athletes, high-performance training, advanced rehabilitation finales
How to Apply BodWave

A Session in Four Steps

BodWave doesn't require special equipment or proprietary software. It's a notation protocol — you can use it in any training system, log, or app.

1

Identify the Muscle Target

Use the MNN v2.0 muscle symbol inside an @ACT() tag for the specific muscle you're training — e.g. @ACT(Glu.Mx:3). This anchors the notation to anatomy — not just to exercise names that vary by gym or coach.

2

Assign a BW Stage

Assess the client or athlete's readiness for this muscle target. Assign BW1, BW2, BW3, or BW4 based on range of motion quality, current load tolerance, and clinical status. When in doubt, start one stage lower.

3

Prescribe with MNN Modifiers

Add joint position via @JNT(), resistance vector via @VEC(), and antagonist compensation via @COMP(). A complete BodWave notation gives the next practitioner everything they need to continue the program without a handoff meeting.

4

Define Advancement Criteria

Write the criteria for moving from this BW stage to the next — specific performance thresholds, pain scores, ROM targets. Progression is earned, not assumed.
